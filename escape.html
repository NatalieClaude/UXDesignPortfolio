Escape the Museum

A Tangible Escape Room with AR for Children

"Escape the Museum" is an interactive, tangible, mixed reality escape room developed as part of my MSc Interactive Media thesis project. The goal was to engage children at the Cork Public Museum through a fun, educational experience, linking the on-site museum visit with an interactive, post-visit app.

The Problem

Museums often struggle to maintain engagement, particularly with younger visitors. The project aimed to create a fun, engaging, and educational post-visit activity that would encourage children to revisit the museum.

The goal was to design an escape room experience that connects both the on-site visit and a post-visit mobile app, using Tangibles and AR to create an immersive experience.

The Process

The design process involved academic research, interviews with museum staff, and observations of visitor behavior. This led to the concept of using Tangibles and AR to link the museum experience with a gamified post-visit activity.

Research

Research: Methods and Insights

I conducted research through interviews with museum staff, observations of museum visitors, and academic case studies. This research revealed the potential for enhancing visitor engagement during the post-visit phase.

Design

Design Solution

The final design centered around a mobile app escape room where children would collect four Tangibles linked to museum artifacts and solve puzzles using AR. These challenges bridged the on-site and post-visit experiences, creating a lasting, engaging connection to the museum.

Further Development

Feedback from parents and educators highlighted areas for improvement, including redesigning Tangibles to be more compact and refining the quiz content to be more age-appropriate for younger children.
